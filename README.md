# The Client Recovery SOP For Agencies

I have lost three accounts to silence and one to a Slack message I should have made a phone call. This is the SOP I built after the fact: 10 conversation stages, 100 scripted lines, 6 ready-to-send templates, a severity routing matrix, and the channel-by-channel rules for when Slack works, when Loom backfires, and when only a phone call will save the account.

## What's Inside

- The 10-stage Recovery SOP: Acknowledgment, Ownership, Balanced Error, Explanation, Damage Assessment, Collaboration, Actions, Communication, Conclusion, Follow-Up. Each stage with 5 openers, 5 closers in agency voice, and the common pitfall operators step on.
- 100 scripted opener and closer lines you can lift word-for-word into your next recovery email, Slack thread, or Loom. Written for agency-owner-to-client voice, not corporate PR voice. Copy, adjust the names, ship.
- A 3-tier severity routing matrix: Tier 1 minor frustration, Tier 2 actively pissed, Tier 3 account at risk. Each tier gets a channel choice, a response window, a written follow-up rule, and a check-in cadence. Stop guessing which fire deserves which response.
- The 2026 channel decision rules: when Slack wins, when email wins, when only a Loom rebuilds trust, when only a phone call saves the account, and the 'no big calls before written notice' rule that has saved every Tier 3 account I have run.
- 6 ready-to-send templates with realistic copy: missed deadline (1-day, 3-day, week-plus late), below-quality work delivered, scope creep collision, communication blackout after going silent, blown campaign or launch, and the full Tier 3 written Recovery Plan template.
- The 12 Mistakes To Never Make in a recovery conversation: using the word 'but', going silent, defensive language, blame-shifting, over-discounting before listening, promising what you cannot deliver, fixing-by-email when a call is needed, and 6 more. Each one paired with the corrective move.
- The Recovery Tracking Scorecard: a one-page weekly check-in template, the 3 signals that mean the account is saved, the moment to declare full recovery, and the 30 / 60 / 90 follow-up cadence post-incident.
- The When to Fire the Client Instead chapter. The 5 signals that mean recovery is no longer worth your time, the exit conversation framework, and the reference move on the way out so you part as professionals.
